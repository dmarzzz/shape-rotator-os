## what is shape rotator

a 10-week program turning cryptography and AI research into products people can use. NYC-based, May 18 – July 18, 2026. cohort 01: ~30-50 builders across 15 invited teams + visitors, anchored at the convent.

run jointly by **IC3 × Flashbots × the convent × blockchain builders fund**.

unlike the canonical YC-style accelerator — closed teams in, closed teams out — shape rotator runs more like a class. you're admitted as an individual or a team, but the program assumes you'll *rotate* through the cohort: ship pieces of other people's projects, get peer-reviewed on yours, occasionally pivot. graduation celebrates **individual growth and project growth** along the same vectors.

the underlying bet: this is a **human capital formation system** as much as a startup accelerator. the target is "high-potential builders to the superhuman stage" — coordinated, AI-augmented, leverage-amplified. projects are one output. rotators who can run their own coordination, evaluate their own PMF, and lead the next thing are the other.

## the cohort

15 invited teams + a rotating set of guest contributors. NYC anchor (the convent). a handful of paper-author advisors from IC3 drop in for Friday research days, demo-day judging, and bounded sprints.

cohort 01 includes both project-track teams (already shipping, came in with a working prototype to take to product) and individual-track contributors (skilled builders without a fixed commitment, expected to find their best-fit slot in the first few weeks).

**participation tiers** (the rituals + rubrics on this page are written for the first two):

- **full shape rotators** — the high-potential individuals, 10-week commitment. peer evaluation, retroactive contribution scoring, the personal API, the journey workbook all apply.
- **project teams** — the 15 invited teams; same 10-week commitment, evaluated as projects on top of individual scoring.
- **guest contributors + sponsors** — bounded participation. onboarding planned for weeks 2-3; tiering, access model, and ritual exposure being finalized.

## the arc

```
weeks 1-2     onboarding · rolling arrivals · define your own success criteria
week 3        IC3 hackathon (princeton) · tale of two cities
week 4        midterm — ETH NY demo day (thu jun 11, external-facing)
weeks 5-8     sprint mode · recombination · projects pivot or consolidate
weeks 9-10    road shows · distribution work · graduation (jul 18)
```

## what we're optimizing for

three success vectors, applied differently per project:

1. **productization** — is this a product people want? PMF indicators, hockey-stick trajectory (organic), distribution-to-market readiness. not "did you implement research IP" — *that's never worked alone*. did you make something useful and get it used.
2. **research grounding & lineage** — is the architecture rooted in something real? IC3 / Flashbots IP picked up and pushed forward, or established mechanisms incorporated outside the team's existing scope. *nice-to-have, not required.*
3. **collaborative contribution** — how much have you helped *other* projects? coordination tooling, peer code review, dataset contribution, design help. retroactively attributed at graduation.

success looks different per cluster: for-profit / commercial-viability track, growth track (delayed gratification, hockey-stick organic), open-source / commons (sustainability + distribution).

## what makes this different

- **research-to-product, not research-to-paper.** the cohort is selected for "researchers / builders willing to ship," not the academic side.
- **rotation, not gatekeeping.** projects can merge, fork, or be dropped *during* the program, not just after. the cohort is the unit, not the team.
- **retroactive attribution.** contributions to other people's projects are scored on graduation, with peer-rubric review.
- **bear-market opportunism.** "50k means something to capable people right now. brokering mergers between projects is possible now and impossible in a bull market." the program incentivizes things the normal VC world under-incentivizes — especially collaboration.
- **the program is itself a coordination experiment.** retroactive attribution, peer rubrics, the commons, personal APIs, shared workbooks — these aren't just logistics. the cohort's experience with them is part of what shape rotator is trying to learn: whether a network effect among connected, agentic builders beats the cookie-cutter accelerator format.
