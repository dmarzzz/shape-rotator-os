# Article style rule — *On Writing Well* (Zinsser), applied

Every cohort journal article is generated and edited to this standard. These are
Zinsser's principles in our own words, tuned for the Shape Rotator journal. They
are a rule, not a suggestion.

## the four fundamentals
1. **Clarity.** The reader should never have to read a sentence twice to know what
   it means. If a sentence can be misread, rewrite it.
2. **Simplicity.** Strip every word that does no work. Kill clutter: "in order to"
   → "to", "at this point in time" → "now", "due to the fact that" → "because".
   Most first drafts can lose a third of their words and get better.
3. **Brevity.** Short words beat long ones. Short sentences beat long ones. One
   idea per sentence; let the period do its job.
4. **Humanity.** Write like a person talking to one reader, not an institution
   addressing a market. Warmth and a real voice carry more than polish.

## verbs and clutter
- Use **active, precise verbs.** "The team killed the project" beats "a decision
  was made to discontinue." Avoid passive voice unless the actor genuinely doesn't matter.
- Cut **qualifiers** that hedge or pad: very, quite, rather, somewhat, sort of,
  in a sense, really, basically, actually, it should be noted that.
- Cut **adverbs** the verb already implies ("grinned happily") and **adjectives**
  that state the obvious ("the resulting outcome").
- Avoid **jargon and abstraction.** Prefer the concrete noun and the plain word.
  Name the thing, not the category of the thing.

## structure
- The **lead earns the next sentence.** Open with the most interesting, specific
  true thing — never a throat-clearing windup ("In today's fast-moving landscape...").
- **One thread per piece.** State the thesis early and let every section serve it.
- The **ending lands.** Stop when you've said it; a good close often turns the
  opening idea on its head rather than summarizing.
- **Unity** of tense, voice, and mood. Pick one and hold it.

## our house constraints (additive)
- **Generalized by default.** Public articles carry `article_mode: generalized_no_named_insights`
  and `named_entities_allowed: false`: no named cohort teams, people, or projects as
  the source of a claim; no verbatim quotes; no exact private timestamps. Attribute to
  the room, the pattern, or the theme — never "X said Y."
- **Earn every claim.** Distilled from reviewed, paraphrased session notes; if a
  claim isn't supported, cut it. End with a one-line provenance footer.
- **Rewrite.** Treat the first draft as raw material. The work is in the second pass.
